As a language learner, I am passionate about crossing cultural and linguistic boundaries, and ultimately about making connections. In 2019, I graduated from the University of Illinois with majors in Economics and Global Studies, minors in Portuguese and Spanish, and a total of four terms spent abroad. I then spent 7 months teaching English in France as I continued to develop my French language proficiency. When the Covid-19 pandemic disrupted my plans in France, I returned to Chicago and began putting my Portuguese language skills to use as a bilingual commodity futures broker. In August of 2021, I plan to begin my deferred Fulbright fellowship to Brazil. While teaching English at a Brazilian university, I hope to get involved in linguistics research projects and in designing better technologies for language documentation and revitalization efforts. In the future, I hope to build upon my quantitative and qualitative research experiences and continue employing intercultural communication to bring beneficial technology and information to people around the world. This personal website is an informal and ongoing exercise in web development using html, CSS, and javascript. As I develop skills in user experience research and design, I plan to update this site with portfolio projects and an improved interface.



"If you talk to a man in a language he understands, that goes to his head. If you talk to him in his language, that goes to his heart." - Nelson Mandela

I speak Portuguese, Spanish and Catalan fluently, and have a working proficiency in French. I also speak German and Italian to varying elementary levels. As a language nerd, I look forward to immersing myself in more languages and cultures for the rest of my life. In an effort to expand my linguistic horizons beyond that of the Indo-European language family, My next language of focus will be Mandarin Chinese.
